	# The Pro. A comic. This is a review.
Just Read the Pro by Garth Ennis, Amanda Connor, and Jimmy Palmiotti. It's genuinely one of the best mature satirical deconstructions of the Superhero genre. I would say that it didn't age very well, but outside of a mention of bombing schools and hospitals, it did.
Ennis's writing is superbly vulgar, but not in a way that's unpalatable. In fact, their vulgarity is tasteful but salacious. The art style is fantastic, Connor's pencils creating a framework for the heavy grunge inks of Palmiotti.
Something like this wouldn't be made today, mostly because they'd release it through 6 issues. Which it kinda was, 72 pages is six 12-page books. The problem comes from pacing it to balance between reading it in one shot and over 6 months. That's the biggest thing.
Though I don't know if Garth would write something like this today. Mostly because it's infused with an anger towards the "Living Gods" of comics for allowing 9/11 to happen. It inspires hope when it proclaims it shouldn't. It's so dense with messages, blatant and subtle.
If there's any one theme, it's "anyone can be a hero, but it sucks ass" and while the first part is blatant and stated in the book, even, it's the second part that takes a deeper reading. I honestly disagree, having hope to spite the world's hopeless state is metal as hell.
